About the program
The program promotes research collaboration among Israeli and Japanese scientists, with support provided by an agreement between the Israel Science Foundation (ISF) and the Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS).


Funding sources
Planning and Budgeting Committee (PBC) of the Council of Higher Education (CHE)

The team
The applications for this program are handled by the ISF team responsible for the International cooperation.


Submission rules
Eligibility: Open to researchers from all types of institutions according to the terms and guidelines for submission and the eligibility guide.
Two proposals are to be submitted:
The Japanese collaborator will submit an application to the JSPS (in Japanese).
The Israeli researcher will submit an administrative application. For full guidelines please go to the Hebrew version.
Areas of research: In this cycle, cooperation in the fields of Social Sciences and Humanities. 
Amount of funding: For the Israeli researcher, a onetime grant of up to NIS 38,000 to be used for up to two years. No overhead is allowed from this grant.
Number of grants per cycle: Up to 5

Review Process
Reviews will be in done in parallel stages and coordinated between the ISF and the JSPS.

Evaluation criteria:

Scientific quality of proposal
Previous experience and scientific achievements of PIs
Essence and benefits of the collaboration

Decision notification
Decisions will be communicated by the ISF to the submitting researcher, with relevant remarks from the review committee.


Grant activation
Grants are activated subject to researcher's approval and signature agreeing to grant terms and conditions.
Annual reports detailing scientific progress and financial expenditure are required and serve as triggers for grant continuation.
A new grant will be activated only after all previous ISF grant's obligations have been concluded.
